## Research interests

Leif's research interests cluster around a core question: *what should AI models do in context, and how do we design for that?*

**Model behavior design**
How AI systems should reason, communicate, and act in constrained, real-world deployment contexts. This includes steerability, intervention design, and the practitioner gap between model capability and appropriate model behavior.

**Interpretability and latent space representation**
Understanding what models are doing internally, particularly as it relates to designing better human-AI interaction. Leif approaches this as a designer and HCI researcher, not a pure ML engineer — he's interested in what interpretability findings mean for how we build AI products.

**Conversational AI and ambient computing**
How conversational AI changes when it moves off the screen and into wearable or AR form factors. Reduced attention, always-on context, embodied interaction — these create fundamentally different design requirements.

**Human-in-the-loop orchestration**
How humans should remain meaningfully in control of agentic AI systems. Leif has been exploring intervention mapping — designing the right moments and modalities for human oversight in multi-step AI workflows.

## Hidden Layer

Hidden Layer is Leif's independent research initiative, conceived as both a skill-development vehicle and a research platform. It focuses on:
